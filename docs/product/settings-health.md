# Settings health and recovery center

Settings is an operational recovery surface, not a catalogue of unrelated forms. The top health check derives ordered findings from typed local state: unavailable system encryption is a blocker, missing or unselected model configuration is required action, and an empty MCP connector registry is optional. Each finding names the consequence and routes to the owning settings section.

The user can rerun the check after changing configuration. Below it, the stable category list uses `aria-current` and the detail area states what the selected section owns. Model settings preserve a list–detail workflow; saving, selecting, testing, and deleting remain distinct actions. Saving an MCP connection never implies that the process has started. Privacy and recovery keeps audit, backup, and restore together without implying that a backup exists when BuBu cannot prove it.

Capability truth remains strict: operating-system encryption availability is reported from the desktop registry, provider and connector counts come from parsed local stores, and recovery readiness is never inferred from a decorative green state.
